Powerful and Continuing Nationalism

Hindutva is an ideology first popularised by Veer Savarkar, an member of the Hindu Mahasabha, in his book titled “Hindutva” in 1923. In it he described Hindu as a nation and established the idea of a Hindu Rashtra. This rashtra he said was to be based on Nazi Germany. Since the rise of the BJP in 2014, Indian Nationalism has been fused with Hindu Nationalism, their introduction into the mainstream has normalised the fusion of the two. The use of a saffron flag, nationalistic songs praising the hindu power of India, slogans like “Bharat Mata ki Jai” and the use of the image of Lord Ram as the icon for a Hindu India are all furthering the idea of a Hindu nation.
